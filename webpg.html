The Minister for Social Justice & Empowerment Shri Thawaarchand Gehlot launched the website for the Nasha Mukt Bharat Abhiyaan (NMBA) on the occasion of International Day Against Drug Abuse and Illicit Trafficking on 26 June 2021, which isobserved worldwide to strengthen action and cooperation in achieving the goal of a sustainable world free of substance abuse. The entire world is facing the menace of drug addiction which has a devastating impact on the addict, individual, family and a large section of society. The launch of Nasha Mukt Bharat Abhiyaan (NMBA) is for 272 Districts across 32 State/Union Territories that have been identified as the most vulnerable in terms of usage of drugs in the country. These vulnerable districts were identified on the basis of findings from the Comprehensive National Survey and the inputs provided by the Narcotics Control Bureau (NCB). According to the National Comprehensive Survey conducted by the Ministry of Social Justice and Empowerment, there are more than 60 million drug users in the country of which a large number of users are in the age group of 10-17 years. OBJECTIVES Nasha Mukt Bharat Abhiyaan intends to reach out to the masses and spread awareness about substance abuse through various activities like: Awareness generation programmes Focus on higher educational Institutions, university campuses and schools Reaching out to the Community and identifying dependent population Focus on counselling and treatment facilities in hospitals and rehabilitation centres that have been geo-tagged Capacity building programmes for service providers INITIATIVES The Ministry of Social Justice and Empowerment has been mandated to reduce drug demand. It coordinates and oversees all aspects of drug abuse prevention including assessment of the extent of the problem, preventive action, treatment and rehabilitation of addicts, dissemination of information, and public awareness and is mandated to run de-addiction centres. Nasha Mukt Bharat Abhiyaan is operational with the involvement of more than 500 voluntary organizations across the country, which are assisted financially under the National Action Plan for Drug Demand Reduction (NAPDDR) scheme of the Ministry of Social Justice & Empowerment. These NGOs have been actively involved in the implementation of Drug-Free India campaign. Nearly 8000 youth volunteers and outreach workers of these institutions/organizations have gone from door to door, village to village and nearby localities, etc. to educate the people about the ill effects of drug abuse and have assisted in the rehabilitation of victims of substance abuse. The Nasha Mukt Bharat Abhiyaan website provides detailed information about the Abhiyaan and its activities, gives glimpses through the Photo & video gallery, provides IEC resource material and information on the institutions set up by the Ministry with the aim of Drug Demand reduction. The "Nasha Mukti Bharat Abhiyaan," also known as the "Anti-Drug Campaign of India," is a comprehensive and multifaceted initiative launched by the Indian government to combat the pervasive issue of substance abuse and addiction in the country. This ambitious campaign encompasses a wide range of strategies, programs, and policies aimed at preventing drug abuse, providing treatment and rehabilitation for addicts, raising awareness about the dangers of drug abuse, and enforcing strict laws against drug trafficking and distribution. One of the primary objectives of the Nasha Mukti Bharat Abhiyaan is to create a society that is free from the clutches of addiction, where individuals can lead healthy, productive lives. To achieve this goal, the campaign focuses on prevention as a cornerstone of its strategy. It includes educational programs and awareness campaigns targeted at schools, colleges, and communities to educate people, especially the youth, about the risks associated with drug abuse. These initiatives aim to instill a sense of responsibility and awareness among individuals, empowering them to make informed decisions and resist peer pressure. In addition to prevention efforts, the campaign places a strong emphasis on treatment and rehabilitation services for those already struggling with addiction. Recognizing addiction as a disease that requires medical attention, the Nasha Mukti Bharat Abhiyaan promotes the establishment and strengthening of de-addiction centers and rehabilitation facilities across the country. These centers offer a range of services, including detoxification, counseling, therapy, and vocational training to help individuals recover from addiction and reintegrate into society as productive citizens. The campaign also addresses the crucial issue of stigmatization and social reintegration of recovering addicts. It recognizes that societal attitudes play a significant role in an individual's journey to recovery and, therefore, strives to reduce the stigma associated with addiction. Community-based support networks and initiatives are promoted to facilitate the reintegration of recovering addicts into their families and communities, allowing them to rebuild their lives with dignity and support. Furthermore, Nasha Mukti Bharat Abhiyaan collaborates with various stakeholders, including non-governmental organizations (NGOs), healthcare professionals, and the private sector, to create a coordinated and synergistic approach to tackling drug addiction. This collaborative effort ensures that resources and expertise are pooled together to provide comprehensive support to those affected by addiction. It also encourages the active involvement of civil society in advocating for drug-free communities and assisting in the implementation of various campaign activities. The campaign's success is contingent on robust law enforcement measures to curb drug trafficking and distribution. Nasha Mukti Bharat Abhiyaan advocates for stringent anti-drug laws and their effective enforcement to disrupt the supply chain of illicit drugs. It collaborates with law enforcement agencies to conduct crackdowns on drug manufacturers, traffickers, and dealers, thereby reducing the availability of drugs in the market. By targeting the root causes of drug addiction and eliminating the sources of illegal substances, the campaign aims to make it more challenging for individuals to access drugs, ultimately reducing the incidence of addiction. The Nasha Mukti Bharat Abhiyaan recognizes that drug addiction is not limited to any specific region or demographic group and, as such, strives to create a national impact. It tailors its programs to address the unique needs and challenges faced by different states and communities across India. This approach ensures that the campaign is adaptable and responsive to the diverse cultural, economic, and social contexts in which drug abuse occurs. As part of its outreach efforts, the campaign leverages various media platforms to disseminate information and engage the public in anti-drug initiatives. Television, radio, social media, and print media are used to reach a wide audience and raise awareness about the consequences of drug abuse. These campaigns often feature testimonials from recovering addicts and their families, showcasing the transformative power of rehabilitation and recovery. The Nasha Mukti Bharat Abhiyaan also places a strong emphasis on research and data collection to continually assess the effectiveness of its programs and policies. Regular evaluations help identify areas that require improvement and provide valuable insights into the evolving nature of drug addiction in India. This evidence-based approach allows the campaign to adapt and refine its strategies over time, ensuring that it remains effective in addressing the ever-changing challenges posed by drug abuse. In conclusion, the Nasha Mukti Bharat Abhiyaan is a comprehensive and determined effort by the Indian government to combat the scourge of drug addiction that has plagued the nation for far too long. Through a multifaceted approach that encompasses prevention, treatment, law enforcement, and community engagement, the campaign seeks to create a drug-free India where individuals can lead healthy, fulfilling lives.
